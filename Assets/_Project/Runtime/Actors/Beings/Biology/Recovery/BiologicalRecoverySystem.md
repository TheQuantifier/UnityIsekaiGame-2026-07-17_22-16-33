# Biological Recovery System

Feature 7.7 adds a body-owned biological recovery runtime. `ActorBodyRuntime` owns `BiologicalRecoveryRuntime` alongside anatomy, body condition, vital processes, hazards, and biological compatibility. Recovery state is tied to the exact Actor/body ID, not the Person ID, so replacement bodies do not inherit old recovery processes.

Recovery Method definitions describe authored recovery behavior such as natural wound closure, tissue healing, fracture healing, organ recovery, Blood restoration, Breath restoration, Fatigue reduction, Sleep Need reduction, Nutrition recovery, Hydration recovery, construct repair, spirit restoration, magical biological healing, holy healing, and necrotic restoration. Biological Recovery Profile definitions decide which methods are available for a Species, body form, or biological classification. Regeneration methods are authored separately from ordinary healing; they only run when a profile, trait, capability, or later source explicitly enables them.

Execution always evaluates Feature 7.6 biological compatibility before recovery starts or ticks. A production caller cannot omit compatibility context and receive permissive recovery: missing or stale compatibility fails closed, and tick requests can carry expected body, condition, vital, hazard, compatibility, and recovery revisions. The runtime does not hardcode Species checks; construct repair, spirit restoration, natural healing, and other future contracts are all mediated by compatibility profiles and interaction definitions.

Structural recovery commits through `BodyConditionRuntime.ApplyStructuralRecovery`. Vital restoration commits through `VitalProcessRuntime.ApplyMutation`. Preview uses the same resolution path but must not mutate recovery process state, condition state, vital state, revisions, dirty flags, or events. Recovery never overwrites condition or vital fields directly, does not remove unrelated injuries or impairments, and does not erase injury history simply because structural integrity improves.

Recovery limits are owned by the Recovery Method and enforced by the Body Condition operation. Natural methods cannot restore authored-absent, destroyed, missing, or severed structures unless the method explicitly allows that state. Natural wound closure and organ recovery stop at their authored partial limits; regeneration, replacement, and permanent-outcome policies are separate authored contracts for later features.

Rest context is represented as body-owned data only: NotResting, Resting, Sleeping, Meditating, RepairStation, SpiritSanctuary, and MagicalRecovery. It does not rely on input, movement, animation, or wall-clock time. Sleep Need requires Sleeping. Fatigue reduction requires an authorized rest context. Construct Repair requires RepairStation. Spirit Restoration requires SpiritSanctuary. Clearing rest context blocks applicable recovery and preserves the process according to the method interruption policy.

Ticks use deterministic allocation. `EqualShare` divides elapsed game time across eligible processes. Priority-style policies such as VitalStructuresFirst, HighestSeverityFirst, LowestSeverityFirst, OldestInjuryFirst, NewestInjuryFirst, PriorityWeighted, and ExplicitTargetOnly sort deterministically and allocate the tick to the first eligible priority process. Equivalent deterministic intervals should produce equivalent results for unfinished processes, and collection insertion order is not used as an implicit priority.

Recovery processes have stable process IDs derived from body, method, target, and transaction identity. Committed transaction IDs protect process creation, and committed tick IDs protect deterministic tick application. Save data persists rest context, process progress, committed transaction IDs, committed tick IDs, and recovery revision.

Restore uses prepare/commit through `ActorBodyRuntime.RestoreFromSaveData`. Recovery restore is silent and does not emit recovery, condition, or vital gameplay events. Saved recovery state must match the exact body and Species and must resolve current Recovery Profile and Recovery Method definitions.

Global Step 6 combat Health is intentionally independent from structural integrity and biological resources. Feature 7.7 does not add a direct Health mutation path inside `BiologicalRecoveryRuntime`; any future method that restores global Health must use `DamageHealingService`, preview through the same boundary, correlate the healing result to the recovery process, and remain duplicate-safe and restore-safe. This keeps body biology from becoming a second owner of combat Health.

Manual testing is available in Test Lab under `Natural Recovery 7.7`. The intended prototype checks are: reset Human recovery, validate readiness, preview wound recovery without mutation, start wound/tissue/fracture/organ recovery, apply ticks, prove duplicate tick protection, prove the natural recovery limit, drain and restore Blood, Breath, Nutrition, and Hydration, add and reduce Fatigue and Sleep Need through rest contexts, verify Construct biological healing is rejected, verify Construct Repair uses RepairStation, verify Spirit Restoration uses SpiritSanctuary, suppress and resume natural recovery through compatibility, and run Save/Load recovery validation.
